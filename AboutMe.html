Hello!

Welcome to my site! My name is Michael Weingert, I am a third year Mechatronics Engineering, Computer Science, and Pure Math student at the University of Waterloo in Canada.

I am an aspiring software engineer with a passion for Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

I've had the opportunity of working in many different software industries including medical, video games, and now business. I am currently working at Microsoft in Redmond, Washington. You can view my linkedIn and github accounts.

You can read about me below, or navigate around for other information.

Growing up in London, Ontario, I had a penchant for math at a young age. I took advanced math through Kumon and took classes at the University of Western Ontario. At my peak, I was the top Kumon student in canada for my age. While studying advanced math, I quickly grew to love the game of chess. I pursued chess for many years, travelling around Ontario for different tournaments. I reached the level of top 10 in ontario for my age. I also began to play competitive soccer and tennis. These sports have continued with me for my whole life.


When I entered the University of Waterloo, I chose to pursue two minors: Computer Science and Pure Math. Through my time at the University of Waterloo, I have had the unique opportunity of taking courses across many discipline. I am on the Dean's Honour List, play several intramural sports including soccer, ultimate frisbee, slo pitch, have represented my residence and class as a mentor for incoming first years, and as a technology representative.


I have had the opportunity to work within several different software engineering fields, including medical, video games, and now business at Microsoft. I am always looking to expand my skills, both through personal projects and work. The more I learn, the more I realize how much there is left to learn. I want to make sure education never holds me back, so I spend my time learning and exploring new technologies, ideas, and theories.
