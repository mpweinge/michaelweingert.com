Hello!

Welcome to my website! My name is Michael Weingert, and I am a fourth year student at the University of Waterloo (Canada) studying Computer Science, Mechatronics Engineering, and Pure Math.

I am an aspiring software engineer with a passion for Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

I've had the opportunity of working in many different software industries including medical, video games, business, and consumer electronics (Kinect/Xbox).

Feel free to view my linkedIn and github accounts, or continue reading about me below!

When I entered the University of Waterloo, I chose to pursue an ambitious work load with an engineering major (Mechatronics Engineering) and two minors: Computer Science and Pure Math. This has given me the awesome opportunity of taking courses across many disciplines. I feel that this coursework gives me a unique viewpoint in the field of computer science.


Outside of school, I make sure to keep myself busy. I play several intramural sports including soccer, ultimate frisbee, and slo pitch. In addition, I have been elected as an academic representative of my class, interacting with professors and board members alike. I also act as a mentor for incoming first years to help them in their transition to university life.


The more I learn, the more I realize how much there is left to learn. As a result, I constantly investigate and explore new technologies, ideas, and theories. Currently, I am conducting research with professors at the University of Waterloo into designing systems to motivate users and encourage useful data collection. I am also looking into expanding touch gesture recognition on mobile devices. I feel as though both of these areas are useful for many different applications.


As my career in computer science continues, I am always looking for unique opportunities to learn. If you feel as though I would be a good fit for a position or if you are just looking to chat, feel free to contact me at mpweingert@gmail.com !
